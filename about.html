How I Think
01 / Philosophy
"How do you approach operations?"
I see operations as the bridge between strategy and execution. When something isn't working, my first instinct is to understand the system behind it — not treat the symptom. I break complex problems into components, find the bottleneck, and design processes that are simple, scalable, and easy for people to follow. The best operations work often goes unnoticed because everything just runs.
02 / Belief
"What do you believe that most people wouldn't expect?"
People are usually the solution, not the problem. When a process breaks down, it's easy to blame individuals — but more often there's a gap in communication, clarity, or system design. I've found that improving the process almost always improves performance without needing to push harder on people.
Working With Me
How I Communicate
I prefer clear and structured communication. For day-to-day work, I lean async — it gives people flexibility and creates a documented trail of decisions. When something needs quick alignment, I prefer a direct conversation to remove ambiguity and keep momentum. My goal is always to communicate early, clearly, and with context.
My Hidden Superpower
Connecting dots across teams. I naturally look for dependencies, risks, and improvement opportunities before they become problems. Whether I'm coordinating a project, managing a CRM process, or supporting operational initiatives — I enjoy bringing clarity to situations where multiple people, priorities, and moving parts are involved.
My Approach to Delivery
I don't just execute tasks — I think about the system around them. Before starting anything, I ask: what's the goal, what could go wrong, who needs to know, and how do we measure it? That habit of thinking upstream saves a lot of firefighting downstream.
What Teams Say About Me
I show up prepared, follow through consistently, and make the people around me feel like things are under control — even when they aren't yet. I take ownership without waiting to be asked, and I'd rather flag a problem early than let it quietly become everyone's problem.
